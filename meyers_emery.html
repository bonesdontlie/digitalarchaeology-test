Hi, I’m Katy!


I am a PhD Candidate at Michigan State University in the Department of Anthropology. I have a strong background in mortuary archaeology, bioarchaeology and anthropology in general. My current focus is on co-occurrence of burial forms, using Anglo-Saxon cemeteries as a case study. I take a strong digital approach, using geographic information services and other digital tools to solve problems in new ways. This summer, I was the Assistant Director for the MSU Campus Archaeology Field School, and helped to develop and lead the course. Over the past year, I was also the Lead Instructor for ANP 203: Introduction to Archaeology, and designed and developed my course from scratch. I also work with the MSU Graduate School to develop and run workshops and bootcamps for graduate students to create and manage their digital identities. I am a research fellow for the Campus Archaeology Program. I am a returning fellow in the NSF-funded Future Academic Scholars in Teaching (FAST) program at MSU.

I am the creator and author of the internationally recognized blog, Bones Don’t Lie, a mortuary archaeology and bioarchaeology news and commentary website. I am also the co-creator and co-editor for GradHacker, an Inside Higher Ed blogwritten for graduate students and by graduate students around the world.

During my tenure at MSU have also served as the secretary and conference organizer for the Council of Graduate Students, have been a fellow in the Cultural Heritage Informatics Initiative twice, and have been Campus Archaeologist, a two year position which involved protection and mitigation of historic and prehistoric sites on MSU’s campus, and have been a teaching assistant.

My goal is to teach anthropology as a full time professor. I would like to focus on archaeology, and run a field school that emphasizes burial or public archaeology, as well as digital methods for sharing and preserving heritage. I am dedicated to improving my teaching, and have been conducting research on my ow teaching over the past year. I hope to continue to merge my interests in mortuary archaeology, digital archaeology and public engagement into my career.

Specialties: Mortuary Archaeology, Osteoarchaeology, Bioarchaeology, Public Archaeology, Historic Archaeology, Digital Heritage and Archaeology, Digital Outreach, Geographic information systems, CMS installation and development, Social media, Website design and development, Archaeological illustration, Teaching, Writing
